Biography

Prof. Pranawachandra Deshmukh (born 1957) is an Indian academic administrator and physicist. He currently serves as ANRF Prime Minister Professor at Central University of Kerala. He was the former Vice-Chancellor of Swami Ramanand Teerth Marathwada University (SRTMU), Nanded.

He earned his M.Sc. in Physics and Ph.D. from Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Marathwada University. With over 42 years of teaching and research experience, he has been instrumental in implementing NEP 2020 reforms, establishing research centres, and promoting inclusive education. He has guided 28 doctoral students and published extensively in condensed matter physics, material science, and higher education governance.

Prof. Deshmukh has served on numerous national committees, including UGC, NAAC, and AICTE, and has received multiple lifetime achievement awards for his contributions to Indian higher education and scientific research.

Present Position: ANRF Prime Minister Professor

ANRF Prime Minister Professor – Central University of Kerala

About the role: The ANRF (Anusandhan National Research Foundation) Prime Minister’s Scheme for Mentoring Young Professors is a prestigious national initiative to bring eminent academicians and former Vice-Chancellors to central universities. These distinguished professors mentor early‑career faculty, design innovative curricula, and lead policy‑oriented research.

Prof. Deshmukh’s responsibilities include: guiding interdisciplinary research in physics and material science, advising the university on NEP 2020 implementation, conducting high‑level workshops for faculty development, and fostering international collaborations. He also teaches advanced courses in condensed matter physics and higher education governance.
